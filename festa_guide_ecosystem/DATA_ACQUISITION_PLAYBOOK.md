# DATA ACQUISITION PLAYBOOK: The Global Scale-Up Strategy

## 1. Scale Objective
- **Target**: 200+ Countries.
- **Volume**: 2,000 $\sim$ 5,000 verified records per country.
- **Total Capacity**: $\approx 400,000 \sim 1,000,000$ high-fidelity festival records.

## 2. Discovery Matrix (Source Mapping)
To avoid bias and gaps, data is acquired from four distinct tiers:
- **Tier 1 (Official)**: National Tourism Boards (NTOs), Ministries of Culture.
- **Tier 2 (Regional)**: Municipal government portals, City Hall archives.
- ** uma Tier 3 (Academic/Institutional)**: Cultural heritage registries, University folklore studies.
- **Tier 4 (Community/Social)**: Verified local community forums, historical society blogs.

## 3. Execution Workflow (Parallel Swarms)
Data acquisition is handled by a **Parallel Agent Swarm**:
- **Regional Leads (Agents)**: One agent per continent $\rightarrow$ further split into agents per country.
- **Scout Agents**: Identify and list all potential source URLs for a specific country.
- **Extractor Agents**: Convert unstructured HTML/PDF into structured JSON objects.
- **Verifier Agents**: Cross-reference data across the four tiers to confirm "Existence" and "Truth".

## 4. Multi-modal Extraction Protocol
We do not stop at text. Each record must be enriched with:
- **Spatial Data**: Latitude/Longitude $\rightarrow$ POI anchoring.
- **Temporal Data**: Exact start/end date $\rightarrow$ Timeline mapping.
- **Visual Assets**: Official logos, festival maps, high-res imagery $\rightarrow$ CDN storage.
- **Documentation**: Research papers, official decrees, and performance reports.

## 5. Cold-Start & Growth Loop
- **Cold-Start**: Prioritize the 'Global Top 100' festivals to establish the baseline.
- **Growth Loop**: Implement a 'Contributor API' where official tourism boards can upload and verify their own data directly to the system, transitioning from 'Crawl' to 'Collaborate'.
