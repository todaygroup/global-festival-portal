# LIFECYCLE RESEARCH FRAMEWORK: The 360-Degree Festival Model

## 1. The Framework Vision
This project redefines a 'festival' as a dynamic process. A festival is not just an event you attend, but a complex organism that evolves through five distinct phases.

## 2. Phase-by-Phase Data Dimensions

### Phase 1: Planning (The Intent)
*Mapping the conceptualization of the event.*
- **Key Data Points**: 
    - Strategic goals (e.g., regional revitalization, cultural preservation).
    - Preliminary budget projections and funding sources.
    - Stakeholder maps (Government $\rightarrow$ Sponsors $\rightarrow$ Local Community).
    - Risk assessment and mitigation plans.

### Phase 2: Preparation (The Logistics)
*Mapping the conversion of intent into physical reality.*
- **Key Data Points**:
    - Vendor and supply chain management scripts.
    - Infrastructure blueprints (Booth layouts, traffic flow maps).
    - Marketing and PR campaigns (Timeline and channel distribution).
    - Staffing and volunteer recruitment data.

### Phase 3: Operation (The Live Event)
*Mapping the real-time pulse of the festival.*
- **Key Data Points**:
    - Real-time footfall data / Crowd density heatmaps.
    - Event schedule execution (Actual vs. Planned).
    - On-site incident reports and response times.
    - Point-of-Sale (POS) and economic transaction spikes.

### Phase 4: Completion (The Settlement)
*Mapping the closure and output.*
- **Key Data Points**:
    - Financial audit and final budget vs. actual.
    - Environmental impact assessments (Waste production, carbon footprint).
    - Site restoration and cleanup reports.

### Phase 5: Analysis (The Legacy & Research)
*Mapping the long-term impact and intellectual value.*
- **Key Data Points**:
    - Participant and visitor satisfaction surveys.
    - Economic impact reports (Direct/Indirect expenditure).
    - Academic research papers on the festival's cultural influence.
    - Institutional memories and historical archives.

## 3. POI Time-Travel Integration
The system integrates these phases into a **Temporal POI Layer** on the map.
- **User Interactivity**: A user clicking a festival POI can toggle between 'Live' view, 'Planning' view, and 'Research' view to understand how the event was built and what its legacy is.
