# USER EXPERIENCE GUIDE: The Hyper-Detail Discovery Portal

## 1. UX Vision
The portal is not a directory, but a **Knowledge-Discovery Engine**. The user should feel they are accessing a 'Global Intelligence Agency for Festivals.'

## 2. Core Interaction Models

### A. The Global POI Map (The a-ha! Moment)
- **Interface**: A high-performance 3D globe/map interface.
- **Interaction**: 
    - **Zoom-in**: As the user zooms into a country, markers change from 'Cluster' $\rightarrow$ 'City' $\rightarrow$ 'Individual Festival'.
    - **Heatmaps**: Option to view festivals by 'Popularity', 'Cultural Significance', or 'Economic Impact'.

### B. The Festival 'Deep-Dive' (The Hyper-Detail)
Upon clicking a POI, the user enters a multi-layered detail view:
- **Layer 1: The Essence**: Name, dates, high-res hero image, and a 1-sentence value proposition.
- **Layer 2: The Experience**: Official links, visitor guides, and multi-modal galleries.
- **Layer 3: The Intelligence**: This is where the 'Lifecycle' comes in.
    - **Planning Tab**: How the festival was designed.
    - **Operation Tab**: Live updates or historical operation logs.
    - **Research Tab**: Downloadable research papers, economic impact reports, and surveys.

### C. The Discovery Engine (Advanced Search)
- **Semantic Search**: "Find me traditional winter festivals in East Asia with a focus on ice sculptures."
- **Comparison Mode**: Contrast two similar festivals (e.g., Venice Carnival vs. Rio Carnival) across the 5 lifecycle dimensions.

## 3. Multi-modal Content Delivery
- **Visuals**: Integrated image carousels and interactive 360-degree panoramas.
- **Docs**: embedded PDF viewer for official reports and academic papers.
- **Video**: Short-form clips of festival highlights linked to specific lifecycle stages.
